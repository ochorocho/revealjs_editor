Iterate {content.main} (a Core\Page\ContentArea, IteratorAggregate over Domain\Record objects) directly with f:for instead of f:render.contentArea. Reason: f:render.contentArea triggers the visual_editor listener that wraps output in a ve-content-area custom element. That element's firstUpdated() callback (Lit) destructively replaces its parent div.slides with itself. By the time that DOM surgery runs, reveal.js has already finished init against the pre-mutation DOM and registered zero slides — nav stays disabled, no slide promoted to .present. f:for emits sections as direct children of div.slides, so reveal.js's ".slides > section" query matches immediately and the deck initialises correctly. Per-slide editing is still visual_editor-aware: each slide CType is wrapped in ve-content-element by tt_content.stdWrap.wrapContentElementsWithVeWrapper=1 (shipped from EXT:visual_editor), and each slide's child column is wrapped via the slide template's f:mark.contentArea.
